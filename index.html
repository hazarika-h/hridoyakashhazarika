Introduction

About Me

In the way branches split and split again, in the rhythm of footsteps fading into distance, in the rise and fall of a breath, there is always some pattern... some order... some invisible structure holding it all together. Even things that seem random carry a kind of hidden consistency as if they are following rules we don’t fully see yet. I think that is why mathematics never feels separate from reality to me. It is there in the symmetry of a face, in the arc of a falling object, in the way time moves forward without ever repeating itself. And the strange part is, we didn’t put it there. We just keep noticing it. The more I pay attention, the more it feels like mathematics is definitely not something we use to describe the world rather something the world is already made of.

As for me, I have always found it hard to believe that things simply “happen” without some underlying order. Somewhere along the way, I chose to study mathematical sciences, though it often feels less like a choice and more like a continuation of this way of thinking.

In all seriousness. I focus on number theory, combinatorics, and mathematical modeling. I am currently working on a few projects and research papers.
